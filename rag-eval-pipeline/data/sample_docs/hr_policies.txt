HEALTHCARE ORGANIZATION HR POLICY MANUAL
Version 3.2 | Effective January 2025

SECTION 1: CERTIFICATION AND COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS

All clinical staff must maintain valid certifications relevant to their role. Expired certifications must be renewed within 30 days of expiry. Staff with expired certifications may not perform duties requiring that certification until renewal is complete.

Basic Life Support (BLS) is required for all clinical staff and must be renewed every two years. Advanced Cardiovascular Life Support (ACLS) is required for ICU, ED, and senior nursing staff and must be renewed every two years. Department heads are responsible for tracking certification expiry dates for their teams and notifying HR 60 days in advance.

SECTION 2: LEAVE POLICY

Full-time employees accrue 1.5 days of paid time off per month, totalling 18 days per year. Sick leave is separate and accrues at 1 day per month up to a maximum of 12 days. Parental leave is 16 weeks fully paid for primary caregivers and 5 weeks for secondary caregivers.

Leave requests must be submitted at least two weeks in advance for planned absences. Emergency leave requests must be communicated to the direct manager as soon as practicable.

SECTION 3: PERFORMANCE REVIEW CYCLE

Performance reviews are conducted twice yearly, in June and December. All staff receive a written review with ratings across clinical competency, teamwork, communication, and professional development. Managers must complete reviews within the two-week review window.

Staff rated below expectations in two consecutive reviews will be placed on a performance improvement plan with a 90-day timeline and bi-weekly check-ins with HR.

SECTION 4: RECRUITMENT AND ONBOARDING

All new clinical hires must complete a criminal background check, reference verification, and credential verification before their start date. Onboarding includes a mandatory two-day orientation covering safety protocols, HIPAA compliance, and system access setup.

Probationary period for all new hires is 90 days. Performance during this period is reviewed at 30, 60, and 90 days by the hiring manager.

SECTION 5: COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS

Salary bands are reviewed annually and adjusted based on market benchmarking. Merit increases are granted during the December review cycle and take effect January 1. All full-time staff are eligible for health, dental, and vision insurance from day one of employment.

Overtime is compensated at 1.5x the base hourly rate for hours worked beyond 40 per week. On-call shifts are compensated at a flat rate per shift in addition to regular compensation.
